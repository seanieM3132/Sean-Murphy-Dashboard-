### **I. Sleep Optimization Protocols**

Quality sleep is the bedrock of mental and physical health, cognitive performance, and immune function [1, 2].

*   **Sunlight Anchoring:**
    *   **Morning:** View bright sunlight within the first 30-60 minutes of waking for 5-30 minutes (longer on cloudy days) [3, 4]. This triggers a cortisol pulse that sets a timer for melatonin release ~16 hours later [5, 6].
    *   **Afternoon:** Get sunlight in the late afternoon/evening. This "low solar angle" light inoculates the retina against the negative effects of artificial light later at night [7, 8].
*   **Temperature Regulation:**
    *   The body must drop **1-3 degrees** to fall and stay deeply asleep [9, 10].
    *   **Sauna/Hot Bath:** Taking a hot bath or sauna 1-2 hours before bed triggers a compensatory cooling response that helps drop core temperature [11, 12].
    *   **Environment:** Keep the room cool and use blankets as needed. Avoid socks if you wake up warm, as the feet are major heat-release portals [13, 14].
*   **Behavioral & Digital Tools:**
    *   **Side Sleeping:** Recommended for efficient **glymphatic clearance** (clearing waste from brain tissue) [15, 16].
    *   **Nose Breathing:** Use medical tape over the mouth at night to force nasal breathing, which reduces snoring and offsets sleep apnea [17, 18].
    *   **NSDR/Hypnosis:** Use Non-Sleep Deep Rest (NSDR) scripts or the **Reveri app** (sleep hypnosis) to fall asleep or return to sleep after waking [19-21].
*   **Sleep Supplement Stack (30-60 mins before bed):**
    *   **Magnesium Threonate:** 145mg (or Magnesium Bisglycinate 200mg) [22, 23].
    *   **Apigenin:** 50mg [23].
    *   **Theanine:** 100-400mg (Avoid if prone to vivid dreams/nightmares) [23, 24].
    *   **Myo-Inositol:** 900mg (Specifically helps with falling back asleep) [25, 26].

### **II. Recovery Techniques**

Recovery is a systematic three-step process: **Inflammation, Proliferation, and Remodeling** [27, 28].

*   **Tapering/D-loading:** Every 8 weeks of hard training, perform a one-week **50% reduction in training volume** while maintaining intensity and frequency to "actualize" adaptations [29, 30].
*   **The "Three Rs" of Post-Exercise:**
    1.  **Rebuild:** Ingest **amino acids** (protein) [31].
    2.  **Rehydrate:** Ingest **125-150%** of fluid weight lost during exercise [32, 33].
    3.  **Replenish:** Ingest **glucose** (carbohydrates) [31].
*   **Lymphatic Drainage:**
    *   **Diaphragmatic Breathing:** Inhale until the belly extends to create a pressure differential that moves lymph from the **cisterna chyli** back to the blood [34, 35].
    *   **Active Recovery:** Walking (7,000+ steps), rebounding on a small trampoline, or swimming to move lymph fluid without a pump [36-38].
*   **Supplements for Tissue Repair:**
    *   **Glutamine:** 20g daily (10g morning/night) to clean up misfolded proteins during proliferation [39].
    *   **Omega-3 Fatty Acids:** 2-3g EPA daily for systemic inflammation [40, 41].
    *   **Creatine Monohydrate:** 3-5g daily for power output and cognitive recovery from sleep deprivation [42, 43].

### **III. Stress Management & HRV Improvement**

Stress tools are grounded in activating the **parasympathetic nervous system** to counter the "fight or flight" response [44].

*   **The Physiological Sigh:** The fastest way to calm down in real-time. Perform a **double inhale (nose) followed by a long exhale (mouth)**. Repeat 1-3 times [45, 46].
*   **Vision Shift:** During high stress or exertion, transition from "tunnel vision" (focal) to **panoramic/dilated gaze**. This signals the brain stem to lower its level of alertness [47].
*   **Cyclic Hyperventilation (Stress Inoculation):** 25-30 deep inhales/exhales followed by a breath-hold. This liberates adrenaline to combat incoming infections (e.g., feeling "run down") [48, 49].
*   **Social Connection:** Investing in trusted relationships releases **serotonin**, which mitigates the long-term effects of chronic stress [50, 51].

### **IV. Caffeine Guidelines**

Caffeine is a potent reinforcer and adenosine antagonist that must be used strategically to avoid performance decrements [52, 53].

*   **Dosage:** **1-3mg per kg** of body weight per sitting [53].
*   **The 90-Minute Rule:** Delay intake for **90-120 minutes after waking** to allow residual adenosine to clear via the natural cortisol pulse, preventing an afternoon crash [54-56].
*   **The "Half-Life" Cutoff:** Avoid caffeine within **8-12 hours** of bedtime due to its long quarter-life (12 hours) [57, 58].
*   **Performance Optimization:** Abstain from caffeine for **2-5 days** (standard) or up to **20 days** (maximal) before a major competition to reset sensitivity [59-61].
*   **Reinforcement:** Pairing caffeine with a specific behavior (like exercise) or food (like broccoli) will subconsciously increase your liking for that activity/taste [62, 63].

### **V. Daily Routine Optimization**

*   **Morning (Wake to 3 hours post-wake):**
    *   Record wake time; your **temperature minimum** is ~2 hours prior [64, 65].
    *   Generate **optic flow** (forward walking/moving) to quiet the amygdala [66, 67].
    *   Fasted work to increase **epinephrine** for focus [68].
*   **Deep Work (The "Tunnel"):**
    *   Use **90-minute ultradian work blocks** [69, 70].
    *   Position screens at **eye level** or slightly higher to increase alertness [69].
    *   Use low-level **white noise** to improve learning/workflow [70].
*   **Nutrition Timing:**
    *   **Lunch:** Lower carbohydrate to maintain alertness/dopamine [71, 72].
    *   **Dinner:** High-starch carbohydrates to increase **serotonin** for sleep transition [73, 74].
*   **Dopamine Management:**
    *   Avoid **dopamine stacking** (e.g., combining pre-workout, loud music, and social media) every session, as it leads to a deeper motivation crash later [75, 76].
    *   Use **intermittent reinforcement**: blunt the reward for intermediate goals to maintain a healthy baseline of motivation [77, 78].
