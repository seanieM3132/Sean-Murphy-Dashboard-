## 1. Pre-Game Preparation

### Circadian Anchoring
- View bright sunlight within **30-60 minutes of waking** for 5-30 minutes to trigger a 50% increase in the morning cortisol pulse, which clears residual adenosine (sleepiness) and sets a timer for melatonin release at night
- Perform a 10-minute walk or light movement (e.g., skipping rope) to increase core body temperature and alertness

### Pre-Game Nutrition
- **Night before (early morning games):** Consume a carbohydrate-heavy meal
- **2-3 hours before:** Eat a meal containing **30-60g of carbohydrates and 20g of protein** (e.g., three eggs and toast)
- **Avoid** heavy meals within 2 hours of play to prevent insulin spikes that cause dizziness and nausea
- **Post-match:** Eat within 1 hour -- carbs + protein at a **3:1 carbohydrate-to-protein ratio** to restore glycogen

### Pre-Game Hydration
| Timing | Amount |
|:---|:---|
| **4 hours before** | ~500 mL of fluid |
| **2 hours before** | 250-300 mL if urine is still dark |
| **20 minutes before** | 200-300 mL of a dilute glucose-electrolyte solution |

### Strategic Caffeine (Pre-Game)
- Ingest **1-3 mg per kilogram of body weight** (e.g., a cup of coffee) approximately **30-50 minutes before** the match to improve reaction time, focus, and power output
- Empty stomach increases effect
- See Section 7 for full caffeine optimization strategy

## 2. Warm-Up & Activation

### Professional Warm-Up Structure (~30 minutes total)
| Phase | Duration | Details |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Aerobic phase | ~10 min | Light jogging, side-stepping, backward running |
| Neuromuscular activation | ~4 min | Glutes, hamstrings, core activation drills |
| Velocity phase | ~2 min | Explosive sprints, jumps, direction changes |
| Ball-specific | ~8 min | Technical ball work, passing, shooting |

### GOATA Groundwork (Joint Decompression & Patterning)
Perform these for **45-60 seconds** each to "recode" natural movement patterns:

| Exercise | Coaching Cues |
|:---|:---|
| **Ground Hip Stretch** | All fours; cross one leg over; keep Inside Ankle Bone High (IABH); head up |
| **Child Rocker** | Toes together, heels out; head up; as butt goes down, ankle bone climbs higher |
| **Toe Tuck Rocker** | Same as child rocker but with toes tucked; ensures heels open during the rock |

### PEP Program (ACL Prevention -- reduces ACL injuries up to 70%)
This is a 15-20 minute routine:

- **Avoidance (Warm-up):** Jog straight (1 min, 3-4 times), side-step shuttle (1 min), backward run (1 min)
- **Flexibility (30 sec/side):** Calf stretch (V-formation), standing quad stretch, figure-4 hamstring stretch, inner thigh stretch, hip flexor stretch
- **Strength:** Walking lunges (1 min), single-leg bridge (30 sec/side), single toe raise (30 sec/side)
- **Plyometrics (30 sec each):** Side-to-side hops over cone, front-back hops, single-leg hops, vertical jumps, scissor jumps. **Cue:** Land softly on the balls of the feet with knees slightly bent

### FIFA 11+ Running Exercises
Perform **two sets** of each exercise:
- **Jog straight:** Keep upper body straight; align hip, knee, and foot
- **Hip Out / Hip In:** Rotate knee while jogging to the first cone
- **Circling Partner / Shoulder Contact:** Shuffle and jump to make contact; land with hips and knees bent
- **Quick Forwards/Backwards:** Run two cones forward, one cone back

### Dynamic Stretches
- Forward lunges, hip openers, heel flicks
- Activation focus: glutes, hamstrings, core
- End with explosive actions: sprints, jumps, direction changes

### Technical & Cognitive Activation
- **Coerver "Pull Push":** Pull the ball back with the sole and push forward with the inside/laces. Work between cones for 20-second speed tests
- **Cognitive Ball Mastery:** Perform "Iniesters" or sole drags while a partner tosses a tennis ball to catch, forcing eyes off the match ball
- **Sensory Activation:** Perform **lateral pelvic tilts** (side-lying, half-kneeling, then split squat) to improve fluid movement and sensation before high-intensity work

## 3. Cool-Down Protocol

### Immediate Post-Match (15-20 minutes)
- **Light jogging/walking:** 5-10 minutes to gradually lower heart rate
- **Static stretching:** 5-10 minutes targeting glutes, hamstrings, quads, groin, and calves

### The "Three Rs" of Post-Game Recovery
1. **Rehydrate:** Drink **125%-150%** of the fluid weight lost (weigh naked before and after)
2. **Rebuild:** Ingest protein (amino acids) within 4 hours
3. **Replenish:** Ingest carbohydrates (glucose) at a **3:1 carbohydrate-to-protein ratio** to restore glycogen

### Lymphatic Drainage
- Use "active recovery" like **swimming or treading water** for 15-20 minutes
- The shearing action of the water on the skin assists in moving lymph fluid and reducing "heavy legs"
- Preferred over static immersion because the water's physics helps "shear" the skin and move lymph fluid

## 4. Ice Bath / Cold Exposure

### Huberman Protocol -- Weekly Target
- **Total: 11 minutes per week**, split into 2-4 sessions of 1-5 minutes each
- **Temperature:** Uncomfortably cold but safe -- **37-55 deg F (3-13 deg C)**
- Move limbs to break the thermal layer for stronger effect

### Soccer-Specific Post-Match Cold Plunge
- **Temperature:** 10-15 deg C (50-59 deg F) for **10-15 minutes** post-match
- Premier League clubs use cold plunge pools routinely (matches every 3-4 days)
- Manchester City and other top clubs have cold plunge pools integrated into recovery areas

### Dopamine & Motivation
- Brief cold exposure can **double baseline dopamine levels** for up to **4-6 hours**
- Best done in the **morning** to stack with the natural morning cortisol peak and provide an energy arc that lasts several hours

### Long Exposure Alternative
- Staying in slightly warmer water (**60 deg F**) up to the neck for **45-60 minutes** is an alternative method to achieve long-lasting baseline dopamine increases

### Memory Enhancement
- Spiking adrenaline via cold water immersion **immediately after a learning bout** can significantly improve retention and reduce the repetitions needed for mastery

### Immune Support
- **Stress Inoculation:** Using cold showers or ice baths to "relax the mind while the body is activated" can raise your stress threshold
- **Combatting Infection:** Acute cold exposure liberates adrenaline, which helps the immune system combat incoming infections

### Critical Timing Rule
- **AVOID cold exposure within 6 hours after hypertrophy/strength training** -- it suppresses the inflammatory response necessary for muscle growth and strength adaptations
- Immediately after training (seconds to hours), the body requires inflammation to heal; avoid anti-inflammatories including ice baths during this window if the goal is adaptation

### Rapid Cooling Technique
- To cool the body down quickly after overheating, focus on the **palms, bottoms of the feet, and upper face** (glabrous skin), which act as heat-release portals

## 5. Sauna / Heat Exposure

### Huberman Sauna Protocol
- **Temperature:** 80-100 deg C (176-212 deg F)
- **Duration:** 15-20 minutes per session
- **Frequency and cardiovascular benefits:**
  - 2-3x/week reduces cardiovascular mortality by **27%**
  - 4-7x/week reduces cardiovascular mortality by **50%**

### Growth Hormone Protocol
- **4 x 30 minutes** at 80 deg C in a single day
- Maximum **once per week** or once per 10 days
- Produces a **16x increase in growth hormone**

### Sleep Optimization (The Cooling Response)
- Take a **sauna, hot bath, or hot shower** for **20-30 minutes** approximately **1-2 hours before bedtime**
- While the heat initially raises your temperature, getting out triggers a **compensatory cooling response**
- The core body temperature must drop by **1-3 degrees** to fall and stay deeply asleep; this protocol accelerates that drop
- **Sleep position:** Sleep on your side to maximize **glymphatic clearance** (brain waste removal)

### Heat Acclimation for Performance
- Regular use of a sauna or jacuzzi can train your "sweating ability"
- Improves sweat rate and helps your body hold onto fluids better, critical for performance in hot or humid environments

### Mitochondrial Health
- Both heat and cold exposure increase **mitochondrial biogenesis** (the creation of new mitochondria)

### Pro Soccer Heat Therapy
- Manchester City uses **infrared sauna** in the recovery area
- Haaland uses a **near-infrared/red-light therapy room** for recovery

## 6. Post-Match Recovery (Next Day & Beyond)

### Immediate (0-1 hours post-match)
1. Light jogging/walking cool-down (5-10 min)
2. Static stretching (5-10 min)
3. Cold plunge: 10-15 deg C for 10-15 minutes (soccer protocol)
4. Meal within 1 hour: carbs + protein at 3:1 ratio
5. Rehydrate 125-150% of fluid lost

### Evening Recovery
- Hot bath/sauna 20-30 minutes, 1-2 hours before bed (triggers compensatory cooling for sleep)
- Sleep on side for optimal glymphatic clearance

### Active Recovery (Next Day)
- Swimming or treading water 15-20 minutes for lymphatic drainage
- Light movement to maintain blood flow without overloading
- Morning cold exposure (1-5 min) for dopamine and alertness boost

### Contrast Therapy Notes
- The inflammation phase immediately after training is necessary for adaptation
- Avoid anti-inflammatories (including ice) during this window if the goal is muscle/strength gains
- For "heavy legs" or swelling, treading water or light swimming is preferred over static immersion

## 7. Caffeine Optimization

### Daily Use (Huberman)
- **Delay 90-120 minutes after waking** most days to allow natural cortisol to clear adenosine first
- **Dose:** 1-3 mg/kg body weight for daily use
- Take **30-50 minutes before match** for peak effect during play

### Performance Dose
- **4-9 mg/kg body mass** for peak performance events
- Empty stomach increases the effect

### Peak Performance Strategy (Tournaments / Key Matches)
- **Abstain from caffeine for 2-5 days before** the event, then resume on match day
- This resets adenosine receptor sensitivity, making the performance dose significantly more effective

### Key Caffeine Benefits for Soccer
- Improved reaction time
- Enhanced focus and concentration
- Greater power output
- Reduced perceived exertion
