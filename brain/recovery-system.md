## Sauna Protocols

### Standard Sauna (15-20 min)
- **Temp:** 80-100°C
- **Duration:** 15-20 min per session
- **Frequency:** 3-5x/week (minimum 2-3x)
- **Benefits:** 2-3x/week = 27% reduced cardiovascular mortality. 4-7x/week = 50%
- **When:** Post-training (NOT post-strength if ice bathing same day), evening pre-bed
- **Pre-bed rule:** Take 1-2 hours before sleep. Triggers compensatory cooling response
- **Care:** Hydrate before and after. Leave if faint. Not within 90 min of bed

### Growth Hormone Protocol (150 min — 1x/WEEK MAX)
- **Temp:** 80°C
- **Structure:** 4 rounds x 30 min with 5 min cool-down between rounds
- **Result:** 16x increase in growth hormone
- **Frequency:** Maximum once per week or once per 10 days
- **Best day:** Wednesday or Sunday
- **Cool-down:** Cool water on palms, feet, face (glabrous skin = heat portals)
- **Post:** Eat protein + carbs within 30 min. Hydrate fully

### Pre-Bed Sauna (Sleep Optimization)
- **Temp:** 80-100°C
- **Duration:** 20-30 min
- **Timing:** 1-2 hours before bed
- **Mechanism:** Core body temperature must drop 1-3°C to sleep deeply. Sauna raises temp, getting out triggers compensatory cooling
- **Sleep position:** Side-sleeping for glymphatic clearance (brain waste removal)

## Ice Bath / Cold Exposure Protocols

### Morning Dopamine Cold Plunge (1-3 min)
- **Temp:** 10-15°C
- **Duration:** 1-3 min
- **When:** Morning (Tue/Thu before team training)
- **Result:** Doubles baseline dopamine for 4-6 hours
- **Technique:** Move limbs to break thermal layer for stronger effect
- **Pair with:** Natural morning cortisol peak for sustained energy arc

### Post-Match Cold Plunge (10-15 min)
- **Temp:** 10-15°C
- **Duration:** 10-15 min
- **When:** Within 1 hour of match end
- **Why:** Post-match = green light for cold (no strength adaptation to protect)
- **Technique:** Immerse to sternum. Move limbs. Breathe steadily

### Post-Sauna Cold Plunge (2-3 min)
- **Temp:** 10-15°C
- **Duration:** 2-3 min
- **When:** After sauna on recovery days (Sunday)
- **Pair with:** Sauna contrast for full cardiovascular benefit

### Cold Shower (Quick Option)
- **Duration:** Last 30-60 seconds of shower
- **When:** Days without ice bath access
- **Technique:** Start warm, finish cold. Shoulders and down

### CRITICAL TIMING RULES
- **NEVER** within 4-6 hours of hypertrophy/strength work — kills inflammatory response needed for adaptation
- **NO** ice bath Friday night — save stress response for Saturday game
- **NO** ice bath Saturday match night — just eat, hydrate, sleep
- **Weekly target:** 11 minutes total, split across 2-4 sessions

## Contrast Therapy
- **Structure:** 3 min hot / 1 min cold x 4 rounds
- **Finish:** ALWAYS finish cold
- **Frequency:** 2x/week
- **When:** Any time (same cold-after-strength rule applies)
- **Why:** Gentler than full immersion. Good for heavy legs and general recovery
- **Not:** Night before a match

## Massage Ball Rolling Routines

### Full Body Routine (22 min)

| Area | Duration | Technique | Care |
|------|----------|-----------|------|
| Feet (plantar) | 2 min | Spikey ball roll sole slowly, 90s each foot. Pause 30s on tender spots | Start here — feet connect to everything |
| Calves | 3 min | Pin-and-stretch: ball under calf, pin spot, flex/extend ankle 10x. 90s each | Hit gastroc AND soleus |
| Glutes | 3 min | Figure-4 position, sit on ball. 90s each side. Find piriformis | Shift weight to locate spots |
| Hip flexors | 2 min | Face down, ball on hip flexor crease. Gentle rocking. 60s each | NOT on bone or organs |
| Adductors | 2 min | Side-lying, ball on inner thigh. Sustained pressure. 60s each | Breathe through it |
| T-spine | 3 min | Double ball peanut (2 balls in sock). Roll slowly segment by segment | Along spine, not ON vertebrae |
| Lower back | 3 min | Ball BESIDE spine (parasagittal), never ON it. 60s each side | Breathe into the pressure |
| Neck/traps | 2 min | Ball between wall and upper trap. Lean in. 45s each side | Jaw stays relaxed |
| Pressure points | 2 min | Find worst spot. Hold 30-45s at 6/10 discomfort. Wait for release | Pain you hold your breath through = too much |

### Region-Specific Quick Routines (5-8 min each)

**Feet & Plantar:** Spikey ball roll sole 90s each → Elevated toe curls 3x20s → Arch pressure with thumb 45s

**Calves:** Foam roll 90s each → Cross-fiber rolling 60s → Spikey ball on soleus/gastroc junction → Massage gun on Achilles (LIGHT)

**Hamstrings:** Foam roll 90s each → Pin-and-stretch (pin spot, extend knee 10x) → Pressure point 45s on tenderest spot

**Glutes:** Spikey ball figure-4 90s each → Find piriformis → Pressure hold 30-45s sustained on trigger points

**Hip Flexors:** Foam roll prone 90s each → Spikey ball on psoas 45s (careful) → Massage gun on TFL 60s → Couch stretch 60s each

**Adductors:** Side-lying spikey ball 60s each → Sustained pressure, not rolling → Foam roll 90s each → Pressure point on gracilis 45s

**Quads:** Foam roll 90s each → Pin-and-stretch (pin spot, bend knee 10x) → Massage gun on VMO + rectus femoris 60s each

**T-spine/Traps:** Double ball peanut 90s → Spikey ball on upper trap against wall 45s each → Foam roll mid-back 60s

**Lower Back:** Spikey ball parasagittal 60s each side → Massage gun on erectors 60s each → Never ON spine

**Shins:** Massage gun along tibialis anterior 60s each → Spikey ball on lateral shin 45s → Never on shin bone

## Lymphatic Drainage Protocols

### Diaphragmatic Breathing (5 min)
- **How:** Deep inhale through nose — belly pushes outward. Long exhale through mouth
- **Why:** Creates pressure differential at cisterna chyli to move lymph back to blood
- **Frequency:** Several times daily. After every session. Before bed
- **Source:** Huberman/Galpin

### Physiological Sigh (1 min — any time)
- **How:** Double inhale through nose → long exhale through mouth
- **Reps:** 1-3 per round
- **Why:** Fastest real-time parasympathetic activation. Calms nervous system instantly
- **When:** Before sleep, during stress, between hard sets, after arguments

### Full Lymphatic Flush (28 min)

| Step | Duration | Details |
|------|----------|---------|
| Diaphragmatic breathing | 5 min | Belly extends on inhale. Pressure differential at cisterna chyli |
| Physiological sighs | 2 min | 3 rounds of double inhale + long exhale |
| Facial lymphatic massage | 3 min | Light pressure: clavicle circles → jaw → under ears → forehead |
| Active walk (nasal breathing) | 15 min | Forward movement pumps lymph. No mouth breathing |
| Rebound/calf raises | 3 min | Trampoline if available, otherwise 3 x 20 calf raises |

### Active Recovery Options for Lymphatic Movement
- **Walking:** 7,000+ steps daily target
- **Swimming/treading water:** 15-20 min. Water shears skin and moves lymph. Better than sitting in a tub
- **Rebounding:** Small trampoline, light bouncing 3-5 min

## Stress Management

### Physiological Sigh
- Double inhale (nose) → long exhale (mouth)
- 1-3 reps. Works in seconds

### Vision Shift
- During stress: shift from tunnel vision to panoramic/dilated gaze
- Signals brain stem to lower alertness

### Cyclic Hyperventilation (Immune Support)
- 25-30 deep inhales/exhales → breath-hold
- Liberates adrenaline. Use when feeling "run down"

### Rapid Cooling
- Palms, soles of feet, upper face = glabrous skin = heat-release portals
- Use cool water on these areas to bring body temp down quickly

## Deload Protocol
- **When:** Every 8 weeks of hard training
- **How:** 50% reduction in training VOLUME
- **Maintain:** Same intensity, same frequency
- **Duration:** 1 week
- **Why:** Adaptations "actualize" during deload. This is when you actually get stronger
