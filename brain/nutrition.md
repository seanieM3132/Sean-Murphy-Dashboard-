# Nutrition, Hydration & Supplements
> Knowledge extracted from NotebookLM video sources

Based on the sources, nutritional excellence for athletes is built upon a foundation of precision **hydration**, **macronutrient management**, and strategic **supplementation** to optimize performance and recovery.

### **I. Hydration and Electrolyte Protocols**

Proper hydration is essential because a body weight reduction of as low as **2% via dehydration** can reduce performance accuracy and increase the perception of exercise difficulty.

*   **Daily Baseline:** Drink **half an ounce of fluid per pound of body weight** daily.
*   **During Exercise (The Galpin Equation):** Ingest your **body weight in pounds divided by 30** (in ounces) every **15 to 20 minutes**. Alternatively, use **2 mL per kilogram of body weight** every 15 minutes.
*   **Post-Exercise Replenishment:** Drink **125% to 150%** of the fluid volume lost during the session. Use a scale to weigh yourself naked before and after a workout; if you lost 2 pounds, drink back 2.5 to 3 pounds of water.
*   **Electrolyte Ratios:** During exercise, use a solution with **200-400 mg of sodium** per serving, ideally maintaining a **3:1 sodium-to-potassium ratio**. Sodium is critical because its depletion causes the "shaky" or anxious feeling often mistaken for low blood sugar.
*   **Timing Tips:** Drink **16-32 ounces of water** first thing in the morning to kickstart the system and avoid the need for excessive nighttime drinking. In the **3 hours preceding sleep**, limit fluid intake to **sipping as needed** to prevent waking up to urinate.

### **II. Macronutrient Strategies and Food Recommendations**

Athletes should prioritize **Whole Foods (Nova Classes 1-3)** and strictly limit **Ultra-Processed Foods (Nova Class 4)**, which typically contain more than four ingredients and are linked to metabolic dysfunction.

*   **Protein Requirements:** Aim for **1 gram of protein per pound of body weight** to support tissue remodeling and recovery. For **young athletes**, the requirement is **1.2 to 1.7 grams per kilogram** of body weight. High-quality sources include **chicken, turkey, fish, eggs, and pasture-fed steak**.
*   **Carbohydrate Fueling:**
    *   **Baseline:** Consume **7 grams of carbohydrates per kilogram** of body weight daily, increasing to **10 grams** in the days leading up to competition.
    *   **Intra-workout:** For exercise lasting over two hours or of extremely high intensity, consume **30-60 grams of carbohydrates per hour**. Use a **2:1 or 3:1 ratio of glucose to fructose** to maximize absorption through separate pathways.
*   **Healthy Fats:** These should comprise **25% to 35%** of daily caloric intake, sourced from **nuts, avocados, and fatty fish**.
*   **The Role of Fiber:** Fiber is the "key to the kingdom" for metabolic health; it forms a protective gel in the intestine that prevents immediate sugar absorption and feeds the **microbiome**.

### **III. Timing and Fueling Strategies**

*   **Pre-Game Meal:** Eat a smaller meal containing **30-60g of carbohydrates and 20g of protein** (e.g., three eggs and a piece of toast) **2 to 3 hours before** competition.
*   **Avoidance Window:** Do not eat a heavy, carbohydrate-rich meal (like a large bowl of pasta) within **2 hours** of high-intensity activity. This causes an **insulin spike** that shunts glucose out of the blood just when your muscles and brain need it most, leading to dizziness and nausea.
*   **Post-Game Refueling:** Refuel within a **4-hour window** after exercise using a **3:1 carbohydrate-to-protein ratio** to maximize glycogen resynthesis and tissue repair.
*   **Sleep and Serotonin:** Eat **starchy carbohydrates** at dinner, as they stimulate the release of **serotonin**, which aids the transition to deep sleep.

### **IV. Targeted Supplement Recommendations**

*   **Creatine Monohydrate:** Take **3 to 5 grams daily**. It is the most research-backed supplement for increasing power output, recovery, and even **cognitive function**.
*   **Caffeine:** The effective dose is **1 to 3 mg per kilogram of body weight**.
    *   **Optimization:** **Delay intake for 90-120 minutes after waking** to allow your natural cortisol pulse to clear residual adenosine, which prevents the "afternoon crash".
    *   **Performance Hack:** For maximum benefit, **abstain from caffeine for 2 to 5 days** before a major competition.
*   **Beta-Alanine:** Take **2 to 5 grams daily** to buffer acid buildup in the muscles and delay fatigue.
*   **Glutamine:** Take **20 grams daily** (split into 10g morning/night) to support the cleanup of dead cells and misfolded proteins during the recovery phase.
*   **Omega-3 Fatty Acids:** Aim for **2 to 5 grams total** (with at least **1,000 mg of EPA**) to support healthy mood and manage systemic inflammation.
