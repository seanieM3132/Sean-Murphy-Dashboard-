### **Weekly Routine Structures**

**Professional Footballer Weekly Structure (Ollie Watkins)**
A typical high-performance week revolves around game days and recovery [1]:
*   **Sunday:** Match day [1].
*   **Monday:** Recovery focus including ice baths, massage therapy, stretching, and flushing legs on a stationary bike [1].
*   **Mid-Week:** Daily performance-specific weight training [2].
*   **Medical Support:** Regular visits to an osteopath (treatment for ~75 minutes) and a sports performance therapist one to two days before a game [1].

**Foundational Fitness Programming (Huberman Protocol)**
A structured approach to long-term health and athletic maintenance [3-6]:
*   **Frequency:** Five days of exercise per week with two full rest days [3, 4].
*   **Duration:** Approximately 60 minutes (±15 minutes) [7].
*   **Programming (The 3:2 Ratio):**
    *   **Block A (10–12 weeks):** 3 days of strength/hypertrophy training; 2 days of endurance training [4].
    *   **Block B (10–12 weeks):** 3 days of endurance training; 2 days of strength/hypertrophy maintenance [5].
*   **Intensity Guidelines:**
    *   **Resistance:** 80% of training should be sub-failure; 20% can be high-intensity training to failure [8].
    *   **Endurance:** 80% of training below the "burn" threshold (Zone 2); 20% pushing past the lactate threshold [9, 10].

### **Footballer-Specific Gym Routines**

**Defensive Midfielder Strength & Power (Nico Gonzalez / Man City)**
Focuses on "neural drive" and moving through a full range of motion [11, 12]:
*   **Leg Press:** Heavy weight (160–170kg), prioritizing full range of motion to recruit glutes and support knee tendons [11-13].
*   **Horizontal Acceleration Push:** Lighter weight focusing on speed of action and horizontal force production; emphasizes a positive shin angle [14].
*   **Ankle Stiffness Drills:** Exercises done with the heel slightly lifted to activate the elastic properties of the Achilles tendon for top speed [15, 16].
*   **Single-Leg Vertical Power:** Vertically-orientated jumps to develop heading and takeoff power while reducing landing load [17].

**Upper Body Routine for Pitch Performance (Matt Stigler)**
Shifts from bodybuilding to athletic movement [18, 19]:
*   **Compound Base:** 1–2 heavy compound movements (e.g., bench press or pull-ups) for 2–8 sets [19, 20].
*   **Accessory Sports-Specific (2–3 sets, 8–20 reps):**
    *   **Alternating Incline Bench:** Loads the rib cage and trains rotation [20].
    *   **Rotation Rows:** Trains the body to rotate from a deceleration position [20].
    *   **Push-up Drop Catches:** Impulse training to teach the nervous system to go from zero to max tension instantly [21].
*   **Speed & Transition (6-Week Progression):**
    *   **Barbell Push Press:** Lighten weight each week to overload speed of transition [22].
    *   **Medball Scoop Tosses:** Challenge speed by increasing approach steps (standing, then 1-step, then 2-step) [22].
    *   **Medball Slams:** Performed in split stances to translate to running mechanics [22].

**Core Specific Routines (Matt Stigler)**
A comprehensive routine to be performed in 2–3 sets for each exercise [23, 24]:
*   **Speed/Rotation:** Fake rotation cross-body throw (split stance) – 3 sets of 5 [23].
*   **Stiffness/Strength:** Heavy Turkish Get-Up – sets of 1 per side, increasing weight until failure [23].
*   **Lateral Flexion:** Cross-leg lateral flexion – 10 reps, then 8, then 6 (increasing weight each set) [23].
*   **Flexion/Extension Pair:** Decline Crunches paired with Thoracic Extension Overhead Crunches – 10–15 reps each [23, 24].

### **Plyometric Exercises & Progressions**

**Weighted Lateral Bounds ( Matt Stigler)**
Personalized based on a "stiffness/flexibility" continuum [25]:
*   **Programming for "Stiff" Players:** Focus on force absorption. 4 weeks of heaviest variation (e.g., 25lbs), followed by 2-week blocks reducing weight by 5lbs; uses deep range of motion [26, 27].
*   **Programming for "Flexible" Players:** Focus on concentric explosion. 2 weeks of moderate weight, followed by 4 weeks of lightest weight; uses short range of motion [28, 29].

**Split Squat Jump Progressions (Matt Stigler)**
A 4-week "extensive" plyometric protocol with 4 sets of 10–20 reps per session [30]:
*   **Speed Focus (Range of Motion Shift):**
    *   **Week 1:** 2 sets Deep, 1 set Mid, 1 set Short ROM [30].
    *   **Week 2:** 1 set Deep, 2 sets Mid, 1 set Short ROM [30].
    *   **Week 3:** 1 set Deep, 1 set Mid, 2 sets Short ROM [30].
    *   **Week 4:** 2 sets Mid, 2 sets Short ROM [30, 31].
*   **Agility Focus (Landing Mechanics):**
    *   **Week 1:** 3 sets Exchange with Pause, 1 set Exchange with Pulse [32].
    *   **Week 2:** 2 sets Pause, 1 set Pulse, 1 set Exchange with Pogo [32].
    *   **Week 4:** 2 sets Pulse, 1 set Pogo, 1 set Regular Split Squat Exchange [33].
*   **Coordination Focus (Planes of Motion):**
    *   **Week 1:** 2 sets Sagittal, 1 set Frontal, 1 set Transverse [34, 35].
    *   **Week 4:** 2 sets Frontal, 2 sets Transverse/Rotational [35].

### **Football Technical Drills (Midfield & Fullback)**

**Midfielder Specific Technical Session (7mlc)**
*   **Warm-up:** 18-yard "Grass Cutter" passes (flat with backspin) using both feet [36, 37].
*   **180-Degree Turns:** Receive ball from rebounder on back foot, turn 180 degrees, and play a through ball to a target [37].
*   **Clipped Passes:** Receive and chip the ball with backspin into "channels" for overlapping runs [38].
*   **Dynamic Ball Mastery:** 30 seconds of non-stop touches through scattered cones using all surfaces of both feet (Repeat 3 times) [39].
*   **Edge-of-Box Finishing:** Pass into rebounder, take one touch out of feet, and strike [39, 40].

**Midfield Control Drills (RC Performance)**
*   **Stick/Cone Passing:** Play ball against a wall, take a touch around a marker, and repeat. Variations: Open the hip, outside of foot, and across the body [41, 42].
*   **Midfield Turns:**
    *   **Turning the Corner:** Spin off a pressing defender using the outside of the foot or Cruyff turn [43, 44].
    *   **Half-Turn:** Receiving in space to transition the ball forward [44].
*   **Distribution Drills:** Figure-8 dribbling followed by an immediate long pass to a target net [45].

**Offensive Fullback Focus (Jeremy Frimpong)**
The training focus for this role is on turning defense into attack [46]:
*   **Defensive Analysis:** Analyzing if an opponent is skillful or fast to set the 1v1 approach [46].
*   **Transition Movement:** Immediately running to free space after winning the ball [47].
*   **Cross Execution:** Targeting the "danger zone" between the penalty spot and the keeper to provide easy finishes for strikers [48].

### **Specialized Tissue & Productivity Protocols**

**Fascia Training (Coach Chong Xie)**
Basic routine to be performed 2–3 times weekly to remodel the "tensity" system [49]:
*   Elevated toe curls [49].
*   HFT (Hyperarch Fascia Training) marble swing [49].
*   HFT gait cycles [49].
*   Fascial adhesion rolling [49].

**Daily Productivity & Habit Formation (Huberman)**
*   **The 90-Minute Work Block:** Focused work in "ultradian cycles." Uses low-level white noise and avoids all internet/phone distractions [50-52].
*   **Physiological Sigh:** A stress-reduction protocol involving a double inhale through the nose followed by a long, extended exhale through the mouth (repeat 1–3 times) [53-55].
*   **21-Day Habit System:** Set 6 new habits per day, expecting to complete 4–5. Use two-day "reset" bins for psychological chunking [56-59].
