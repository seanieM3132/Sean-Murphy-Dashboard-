## 3. Hips & Hammys Program

| Exercise | Sets x Reps x Seconds | Notes |
|----------|----------------------|-------|
| A1. Hip Flexor Leg Lifts | 3x3x5" | Keep the leg straight and back hard. Ensure working leg's heel stays in line with big toe throughout. Add 1-2kg to progress |
| A2. Banded Hamstring Stretch | 3x3x5" | Keep the knee locked and allow the hip flexor to pull the leg into flexion, stretching the hamstring |
| Hamstring Block Crush | 1x3x10" | Heel must connect to the yoga block or object. Once consistent, add sets to build endurance in the short range. Override cramps to build strength |
| Hip 90/90 IR | 3x3x5" | Maintain strong posture without leaning away from the working leg. Add 1-3kg if unloaded reps are too easy |
| Butterfly C.R.A.C | 3x6x10" | Keep contractions strong. Increase weight as you feel safe, building to 25% body weight |
| Banded Hip Flexion End-Range Iso | 1x3x10" | Strive to hold maximum end range of hip flexion. Increase time under tension and band resistance to progress. Start with the lightest band |
| Lateral Hip Flexion | 3x3x5" | Stay centered without leaning away from the working leg. Add weight as reps become easier |
| Wall Frog + Hip IR | 3x4x5" | Keep the hips back and shoulders above the hips. Add 1-5kg ankle weights if rotations are easy and form is solid |
| Couch Stretch/Hip Flexor Stretch | 2x1 min | Keep the quad straight during couch stretch and bring the knee close to the wall. Use an adjustable bench if the wall is too intense |

**Progression Tips:**
- Use ankle weights, bands, or external loads as noted to progressively increase difficulty
- Focus on form and control over adding weight too quickly
- If experiencing cramps, try to work through them to develop strength in new ranges

## 4. Nordic Program (21+ Week Progression)

| Phase | Exercise | Weeks | Sets | Reps | Tempo | Progression |
|-------|----------|-------|------|------|-------|-------------|
| Phase 1 | Banded Assisted Nordic Curls | Weeks 1-8 | 3-4 | 5-8 | 3-4 seconds | Start with moderate resistance band, gradually reduce band assistance as strength improves |
| Phase 2 | Partial Eccentric Nordic Curls | Weeks 9-12 | 3-4 | 3-8 | 3-5 seconds | Limit range using props like foam rollers, gradually reduce prop height as strength improves |
| Phase 3 | Full Negative Nordic Curls | Weeks 13-16 | 3 | 3-5 | 4-6 seconds | Perform slow, controlled descents with assistance to return to the starting position |
| Phase 4 | Eccentric-Concentric Nordic Curls (Partial) | Weeks 17-20+ | 3-4 | 3-6 | 3-4 seconds | Combine eccentric lowering with explosive concentric phase; focus on partial range before progressing to full reps |
| Phase 5 | Full Nordic Curls | Weeks 21+ | 3 | 3-5 | Controlled | Progress to full unassisted reps, gradually increase rep volume as control improves |

## 6. Stronger Calves & Ankle Program

| # | Exercise | Sets/Reps | Purpose / Benefit |
|---|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 | Slant board unilateral calf raise | 3x8-10 build to 20% BW (if achieved, go 25-35% BW) | Builds gastrocnemius strength, ankle stability, reinforces Achilles and plantarflexion |
| 2 | Bent-leg unilateral calf raise | 3x8-10 build to 20% BW | Targets soleus for endurance and ankle support; improves midfoot stability |
| 3 | Tib raises | 3x8-10 at 10% BW (if achieved, build to 15%) | Strengthens tibialis anterior for dorsiflexion control and shin stability |
| 4a | Foot stretch (Superset A1) | 60s / 40s / 20s | Improves plantar fascia mobility and foot mechanics |
| 4b | Hack squat (Superset A2) | 3x8 | Builds lower-leg, ankle, and knee synergy |
| 5 | Banded ankle dorsiflexion | 2 min | Improves dorsiflexion by assisting posterior talus glide |
| 6 | Deep squat — heels elevated | 4 min in as few sets as possible | Improves ankle dorsiflexion, knee tracking, and hip mobility under load |
| 7 | Elevated seiza | 1 min | Stretches calves, improves ankle flexibility, strengthens foot arches |
