Two Types of Cold

DopamineMorning standalone cold (1-3 min). Doubles baseline dopamine for 4-6 hours. Best stacked with morning cortisol peak. Move limbs to break thermal layer.

RecoveryPost-match cold plunge (10-15 min at 10-15C). Soccer-specific protocol for when you play every 3-4 days. Premier League standard. Longer duration than dopamine sessions.

Key Facts

Stress inoculation: relax mind while body is activated = raises your stress threshold over time. Builds mental toughness for high-pressure matches.

Immune support: acute cold exposure liberates adrenaline, helping immune system combat incoming infections when feeling "run down."

Immediate Post-Match

0-1 hour after final whistle

  1. Light jog/walk cool-down5-10 min. Bring heart rate down gradually. Don't just stop.
  2. Static stretching: glutes, hammys, quads, groin, calves30 seconds each side. 5-10 min total. Address anything that feels tight.
  3. Cold plunge: 10-15C for 10-15 minSoccer-specific protocol (longer than morning dopamine sessions). Premier League standard for match recovery.
  4. Protein shake + banana immediatelyStart the rebuild process. Don't wait until you get home.
  5. Meal within 1 hour: 3:1 carb-to-protein ratioE.g. rice + chicken, pasta + mince. Replenish glycogen ASAP.
  6. Rehydrate: 125-150% of fluid lostWeigh before and after match. Every kg lost = 1.25-1.5L to drink back.

The Three Rs

R REHYDRATE

125-150% of fluid weight lost. Weigh before/after. Electrolytes, not just water.

R REBUILD

Protein (amino acids) within 1 hour. 30-40g. Shake or real food.

R REPLENISH

Carbs at 3:1 ratio to refill glycogen. Rice, pasta, potatoes.

Sleep Architecture

9 hrsTarget: 10pm lights out, 7am wake. 9 hours in bed = optimal for testosterone, growth hormone, and full recovery.

1-3FCore temp must DROP 1-3 degrees to fall and stay deeply asleep. Sauna 1-2hrs before bed triggers compensatory cooling.

50%Bright light 10pm-4am suppresses dopamine by 50% for DAYS. Dim/warm lights only after 8pm.

Sleep Protocol

  1. Sunlight anchoring: 10+ min morning light sets melatonin timer ~16 hours laterAlso get late afternoon sunlight to inoculate retina against artificial light at night.
  2. Room: cool, dark, quietKeep room cool. Use blankets as needed. Feet are heat-release portals.
  3. Side sleeping for glymphatic clearanceClears brain waste most efficiently. Nose tape for nasal breathing.
  4. Sauna/hot bath 1-2hrs before bed (Tue/Thu/Fri)Triggers compensatory cooling response. Core temp drops for deeper sleep.
  5. Sleep supplements 30-60 min before bedMg Threonate 145mg + Apigenin 50mg + L-Theanine 100-400mg. Myo-inositol 900mg if waking mid-night.

NSDR (Non-Sleep Deep Rest)

NSDRIf you can't sleep within 20 min: do a 10-min NSDR protocol (YouTube "Huberman NSDR"). Don't lie there stressing. Also useful for afternoon recovery without full nap.

ReveriHuberman's recommended app for sleep hypnosis scripts. Free option: YouTube NSDR scripts. Use when mind is racing pre-game.

Stress Management

SighPhysiological Sigh: double inhale (nose) + long exhale (mouth). Fastest real-time calm-down tool. Use pre-game, at half-time, before penalties.

VisionDuring high stress: shift from tunnel/focal vision to panoramic/dilated gaze. Signals brainstem to lower alertness. Use when anxious before kickoff.

Caffeine Strategy

90 minDelay caffeine 90-120 min after waking. Let natural cortisol clear adenosine first. Prevents afternoon crash. (Huberman)

2-5 daysAbstain from caffeine 2-5 days before key matches. Resets adenosine receptor sensitivity. Match-day dose hits significantly harder.

8-12 hrsHalf-life cutoff: avoid caffeine within 8-12 hours of bedtime. Quarter-life is 12 hours. Even afternoon coffee can wreck sleep architecture.

Recovery Nutrition Timing

LunchLower carb for alertness/dopamine during the day. Protein + veg + moderate carbs.

DinnerHigh-starch carbs (pasta, rice, potatoes) to increase serotonin for sleep transition. Protein + carbs + veg.

DeloadEvery 8 weeks: 50% reduction in training volume for 1 week while maintaining intensity. "Actualize" your adaptations. (Galpin)
