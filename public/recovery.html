CRITICAL RULE

AVOID cold within 6 hours after gym. It suppresses the inflammatory response needed for muscle growth.

Key Facts

2xDoubles baseline dopamine for 4-6 hours. Morning = best timing to stack with cortisol peak.

Move limbs to break thermal layer for stronger effect. Don't stay still — agitate the water around you.

Spiking adrenaline after learning improves retention. Cold exposure post-study = better memory consolidation.

Rapid cooling targets: palms, soles of feet, upper face (glabrous skin). Use these for quick cooldowns.

Stress inoculation: relax mind while body is activated = raises your stress threshold over time. Builds mental toughness.

NEVER within 6 hours of strength/hypertrophy work — kills the inflammatory adaptation signal your muscles need to grow.

Immediate Post-Match

0-1 hour after final whistle

  1. Light jog/walk cool-down5-10 min. Bring heart rate down gradually.
  2. Static stretching: glutes, hammys, quads, groin, calves30 seconds each. 5-10 min total.
  3. Cold plunge: 10-15C for 10-15 minSoccer-specific protocol. Longer than standalone sessions.
  4. Meal within 1 hour: 3:1 carb-to-protein ratioE.g. rice + chicken, pasta + mince, recovery shake + banana.
  5. Rehydrate: 125-150% of fluid lostWeigh before and after match to calculate.

The Three Rs

R REHYDRATE

125-150% of fluid weight lost. Weigh before/after.

R REBUILD

Protein (amino acids) within 4 hours post-match.

R REPLENISH

Carbs at 3:1 carb-to-protein ratio to refill glycogen.

Next Day (Sunday)

  1. Swimming/treading water 15-20 minLymphatic drainage. Shearing action of water moves lymph.
  2. Light movement for blood flowWalk, easy bike, nothing that creates soreness.
  3. Morning cold exposure 1-3 min for dopamineStandalone. Not post-gym.
  4. 112mvmnt: Hips & Hammys routineMobility focus. Restore range of motion.
IF SUBBED EARLY / <60 MIN PLAYED

Do a 20-min tempo run at 70% OR 6x100m sprints with 45s rest. Log it so Sunday recovery is adjusted accordingly.
